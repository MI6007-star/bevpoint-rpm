The Opportunity
The track is the soul. The hospitality is what keeps members living in it.

The members writing $800K–$1.2M checks aren't buying laps — they're buying a world built around the track. Race in the morning, host clients at lunch, entertain a spouse at dinner, wake up ready to do it again. The restaurant, hotel, and events don't compete with the track experience. They extend it.

Our Philosophy
Paddock culture. Not generic luxury.

The biggest mistake a motor club makes is hiring a hotel company to design its restaurant and a corporate caterer to run its events. The result is exactly what you'd expect: forgettable. We don't let the hospitality contradict the brand.

01

Authentic over aspirational

Don't design a "luxury club restaurant." Design the place where drivers eat after a great session and invite their people. Grit + greatness, not white gloves.

02

One brand, every touchpoint

The menu, the room, the overnight experience, the morning coffee — they all have to feel like they came from the same hand. Inconsistency is the most expensive thing you can build.

03

Revenue that earns its room

Every hospitality decision should serve two masters: the member experience and the P&L. Beautiful concepts that don't generate returns aren't hospitality — they're overhead.
