The Opportunity
The track is the soul. The hospitality is what keeps members living in it.

The members writing $800K–$1.2M checks aren't buying laps — they're buying a world built around the track. Race in the morning, host clients at lunch, entertain a spouse at dinner, wake up ready to do it again. The restaurant, hotel, and events don't compete with the track experience. They extend it.

What makes a motor club destination-grade

  • A restaurant worth a special trip — not just "the place you eat when you're at the track"
  • Lodging that completes the overnight experience — not just a place to sleep between sessions
  • An events calendar that gives members 52 reasons to come back — not just track day weekends
  • A brand so cohesive that the restaurant, the track, and the garage loft all feel like chapters in one story

Revenue beyond founding memberships

F&B Revenue Member dining, private events, guest visits. A flagship restaurant running 60 covers/night adds $1M–$2M+ annually at this tier.
Lodging Revenue Overnight guest fees, weekend packages, corporate buyouts. Drives average spend-per-visit up 3–5×.
Events & Partnerships Brand partnerships, automotive lifestyle weekends, private club buyouts. Revenue that compounds with reputation.
Retention Value Members who use the club 3+ times/month for non-track reasons have 94% renewal rates at comparable clubs.
Our Philosophy
Paddock culture. Not generic luxury.

The biggest mistake a motor club makes is hiring a hotel company to design its restaurant and a corporate caterer to run its events. The result is exactly what you'd expect: forgettable. We don't let the hospitality contradict the brand.

01

Authentic over aspirational

Don't design a "luxury club restaurant." Design the place where drivers eat after a great session and invite their people. Grit + greatness, not white gloves.

02

One brand, every touchpoint

The menu, the room, the overnight experience, the morning coffee — they all have to feel like they came from the same hand. Inconsistency is the most expensive thing you can build.

03

Revenue that earns its room

Every hospitality decision should serve two masters: the member experience and the P&L. Beautiful concepts that don't generate returns aren't hospitality — they're overhead.

"The Thermal Club doesn't have a hotel. Monticello doesn't have a destination restaurant. That's the gap — and Roaring Pines fills it. One property where you land, race, eat, stay, and do it again. There's nothing like it in the Southeast."

— Bevpoint Strategic Positioning
